Avoid costly late fees by renewing your real estate license on time. Late renewals within two years of expiration will result in a $525 fee for salespeople and a $675 fee for brokers, significantly higher than the on-time rates. While you can renew within this two-year grace period without retaking the licensing exam, you are prohibited from practicing real estate during this time. If you fail to renew within two years, your license will expire permanently, and you will be required to retake the real estate licensing exam and requalify. To avoid these penalties and ensure uninterrupted practice, we strongly recommend renewing your license on time.
